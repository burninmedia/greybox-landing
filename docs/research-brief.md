# 🔍 Agent Audit Trail — Research Brief
**March 21, 2026**

---

## 📊 Competitive Landscape

**Observability tools** (LangSmith, Helicone, Langfuse, AgentOps) — developer-focused, debugging toys, not compliance-ready. $0-$50/mo.

**Enterprise GRC** (Credo AI, Rubrik, IBM watsonx) — $100K+/yr, enterprise-only, compliance-team toys.

**The gap:** No one owns "audit trail for agent decisions." Mid-market is wide open.

| Tool | What it is | Audit Gap |
|---|---|---|
| LangSmith | LLM observability | Debugging, not compliance |
| Helicone | Proxy-layer logging | Events, no decision trees |
| Credo AI | Enterprise governance | $100K+/yr, not agent-native |
| Zenity | Security + governance | Threat detection framing |

---

## 📈 Market Timing

• AI agents: **$7B (2025) → $93B by 2032** (44.6% CAGR)
• Meta acquired MoltBook March 2026 — top-of-market validation of agent identity/audit as a category
• EU AI Act high-risk requirements: **August 2026**, fines up to €35M or 7% global turnover
• Agentic AI startups raised **$2.8B in H1 2025**

**Verdict:** Category is hot. Window is open. Must move before incumbents add compliance layers.

## 🎯 Pain Points Solved

1. Can't explain agent decisions to regulators
2. Can't get legal sign-off for regulated industries
3. Can't satisfy GDPR subject-access requests
4. Debugging agent failures at 2am is brutal
5. Enterprise customers demand audit interfaces — nothing to give them
